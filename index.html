I absolutely have, and I've always found that how you frame it has a huge impact on how you feel about it. It allows you to move on. I told him the way I've approached this sort of thing in the past is to frame it as "overall, this job cost more than I expected", rather than "This guy fucked me, I'm out $X, and now I have to pay someone else $Y more to redo it". Yes, the total is the same, but one involves loss while the other involves missed expectations. I find it a lot easier to deal with underestimating total cost than feeling like I got screwed.
